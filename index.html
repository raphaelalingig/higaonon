Want to know more about the Higaonon Tribe? The Higaonon (also Higa-unon or Higaonon) are one of the indigenous Lumad peoples of northern and central Mindanao, Philippines, known as "people of the living mountains" or "people of the wilderness" from Binukid words higa (living), goan (mountains), and onon (people). They inhabit highlands across Misamis Oriental (including Cagayan de Oro's Tablon/Baloy and Nahilaran/Dansolihon ancestral domains), Bukidnon, Agusan del Sur/Norte, Lanao del Norte, and Camiguin, with populations estimated at 100,000+ in Misamis Oriental-CDO areas alone.

Our Language

Traditional Dances

Dances from Nahilaran: Preservation-focused dances including Dugso (spiritual stomping/jumping for thanksgiving), Binaylan (shaman-led handkerchief waves evading hawk), and Anahaw (prayerful fan-like arm waves). Taught weekly to youth; featured in rituals and school festivals with beadwork, anklets, and symbolic colors (white for purity).

Pugas

A cleansing or rice-related ritual dance (from "pugas" meaning husked rice), involving offering gestures and stomping to symbolize purification and thanksgiving for harvest abundance.

Duwaya

An imitative or courtship dance mimicking gentle movements (possibly bird or nature-inspired), performed in pairs or groups with flowing arm waves and handkerchief props for entertainment during gatherings.

Tagmalaki

A warrior or "big man" dance honoring brave leaders (bagani), featuring strong, aggressive steps with shields/spears, red attire for bravery, and gong rhythms to depict strength and protection of the tribe.

Panaghiusa

A unity dance (from "panaghiusa" meaning oneness), performed in large circles with hand-holding formations symbolizing tribal harmony, community bonds, and peace pacts; often closes rituals with synchronized stomps and anklet sounds.

Dances from Tablon: Ritual and social dances like Dinugso (women stomping in circles around fire for healing, no music), Saut (warriors with shields/spears in aggressive strikes), and Binanog (hawk imitation gliding/chasing). Performed in initiations, birthdays, and Higalaay street dancing with gongs, red attire, and group unity formations.

Binanog

Binanogv2

Binanog: Binanog (or Binanog Banog) is an entertaining imitative dance performed by the Higaonon in Tablon, mimicking the banog (hawk) in flight, gliding, swooping, courting, or hunting/chasing prey. Often depicts a male hawk pursuing a female, or a hawk chasing a hen protecting chicks.

Dalinday Sa Binukid

A traditional Higaonon limbay (lyric folk poem/song) in Binukid language, often sung as a melancholic ballad expressing love for the mountains ("sa binukid" meaning "in the highlands"). It evokes nostalgia for ancestral lands, nature's beauty, and simple mountain life; performed during rituals, gatherings, or storytelling in communities like Tablon/Nahilaran to preserve oral heritage.

Asom

A kaliga-on ceremonial song in Binukid, typically part of the "Song of the Rejected Lover" genre. "Asom" refers to sorrow, longing, or heartache (e.g., "anlo asom" meaning deep sadness); archaic lyrics depict unrequited love, rejection, and emotional pain, sung by baylan (shamans) and groups during healing rituals or poetic discourses for spiritual catharsis.
