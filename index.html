Our Land

[PLACEHOLDER] Description of the mountainous regions and natural environment of North-Central Mindanao. The Higaonon people have lived in these majestic mountains for generations, developing a deep connection with the land. The terrain includes lush forests, flowing rivers, and diverse ecosystems that have sustained our communities. Our ancestral domain spans across multiple provinces, each with its unique landscape and resources that we have learned to steward responsibly.

Our People

[PLACEHOLDER] Information about the Higaonon community, population, and social structure. The Higaonon are known for their strong sense of community and traditional governance systems. Our social structure is built on respect for elders, collective decision-making, and the preservation of cultural values. Families are extended and interconnected, creating a network of support and shared responsibility across generations.

Our Language

[PLACEHOLDER] Details about the Higaonon language, dialects, and linguistic heritage. The Higaonon language is part of the Manobo language family and serves as a vital link to our cultural identity. Our language carries the wisdom of our ancestors, traditional stories, and the knowledge of our environment. Efforts are ongoing to preserve and teach the language to younger generations to ensure our cultural continuity.
